# Spec: Courts

## Summary
The main content area displaying all active badminton courts. Courts can be added, configured, and deleted. Players are dragged into court slots; matches are resolved with win/loss attribution.

## Functional Requirements
- **Default setup**: 3 doubles courts (size 4, 4 slots each) generated by `seed()` on first load.
- Each court has: a unique ID, a court number, a size (2 = singles, 4 = doubles), and an ordered slot array of player IDs or `null`.
- **Add Court**: "+ Court" button in the masthead opens the Add Court dialog (choose Singles or Doubles).
- **Remove Court**: Requires a 2-click confirmation dialog. Removing a court returns all its players to Idle.
- **Court size (singles/doubles)** can be changed via the Edit Court dialog; slots are preserved up to the new size and truncated or extended as needed.
- Players can be dragged from the sidebar or queue into any court slot (`assignToCourtSlot`).
  - If the target slot is occupied, the existing player is displaced to Idle.
  - Assigning a player releases them from any previous court or queue slot.
- Individual court slots can be cleared (`releaseCourtSlot`), returning the player to Idle.
- Queue cards can be dragged onto a court to bulk-promote all queued players (`promoteQueueToCourt`).
  - Players already on the court are displaced to Idle.
- **Finish Match**: A court with at least one player on each side shows a "Finish" action.
  - User selects: **Team A wins**, **Team B wins**, or **Draw / No result**.
  - On finish: all players on the court move to Idle, `gamesPlayed` increments by 1, winners get `+1 win`, losers get `+1 loss` (Draw = no W/L change).
  - Court slots are cleared after the match is resolved.
- **Clear Session**: "Clear Session" button in the masthead resets the entire session to the default 3 doubles courts with no players (`resetAll()`). Uses a 2-click confirmation pattern with a 3 s auto-reset.

## Match Timer
- Each court tracks a `matchStartedAt?: number` timestamp in the store.
- `matchStartedAt` is set to `Date.now()` when a court transitions from vacant → ongoing (both sides have at least one player).
- When the court is already ongoing and a player swap occurs (e.g., displacing one player and placing another), `matchStartedAt` is preserved — the clock is not restarted.
- `matchStartedAt` is cleared (`undefined`) when the court goes vacant or a match is finished.
- While ongoing, the court card header displays a live **MM:SS** match timer in `font-mono digit tabular-nums` style:
  - **Neutral** (`text-bone-3`): < 12 minutes.
  - **Amber** (`text-warm`): ≥ 12 minutes.
  - **Red** (`text-alert`): ≥ 15 minutes.
- The timer replaces the "Singles / Doubles" label in the header when a match is active.

## Matches Completed Counter
- `session.matchesCompleted: number` tracks how many matches have been resolved with a declared winner.
- Incremented by `finishMatch` only when `winner === "A"` or `winner === "B"`. Draw / No result does not increment.
- Displayed in the metric bar under The Floor → **Completed**.
- Initialized to `0` in `seed()`.

## Court Visual Design
- **Surface**: BWF-accurate green (`#1a7a40`) with a subtle top highlight gradient.
- **Boundary lines**: 2px white lines on the left and right edges of the court surface via CSS `background-image` gradient strips (`rgba(255,255,255,0.88)`).
- **Net / centre divider**: 2px `border-x` white (`border-white/70`), visually splitting Team A from Team B.
- **Court label**: "Court N" displayed in the card header, left-aligned.
- **Active state**: when both sides have at least one player, the court is considered "Ongoing" — the match timer replaces the format label in the header.

## Player Slot Layout
- **Background**: bone card (`bg-[#f0f2f5]`), `border-[0.5px] border-black/10` — high contrast against the green surface.
- **Content (left to right)**: `♂/♀` gender icon (color-coded) · player name (truncated, semibold) · level chip (ranking tier, solid opaque via `.court-surface .lvl-*` overrides).
- **Empty slot**: dashed border placeholder, drop-target highlight on hover during drag.
- **Cursor**: `cursor-grab` at rest, `cursor-grabbing` while dragging.

## Level Chip Colors on Court Surface
Chips use fully opaque `rgb()` backgrounds (not semi-transparent) to maintain contrast on the green surface:

| Level | Background | Text |
|---|---|---|
| Beginner | `rgb(160, 88, 34)` | `#ffd4a0` |
| Low-Intermediate | `rgb(90, 100, 118)` | `#e0e8f4` |
| Intermediate | `rgb(158, 122, 16)` | `#fff0a0` |
| Upper-Intermediate | `rgb(24, 138, 174)` | `#c0f0ff` |
| Advanced | `rgb(48, 82, 200)` | `#c0d4ff` |
| Professional | `rgb(118, 38, 178)` | `#f0c0ff` |

## UI/UX Considerations
1. **Court grid**: courts render in a responsive grid; scrollable vertically on overflow.
2. **Displacement**: dropping onto an occupied slot silently displaces the occupant to Idle — no confirmation needed, as this is expected workflow.
3. **Finish dialog**: prompts "Who won?" with Team A / Team B / No Result — disabled if the court has fewer than 2 players total or an unequal side count.
4. **Court deletion confirmation**: modal or inline 2-click confirm before removing a court mid-match.
5. **Add Court dialog**: choose Singles (1v1) or Doubles (2v2) — creates court with appropriate slot count.
6. **Empty courts**: show placeholder slots with dashed borders and "Drop Player" label.

## Edge Cases
1. **Incomplete match finish**: Finish button disabled when fewer than 1 player per side.
2. **Court deletion with active players**: confirmation required; all players return to Idle on confirm.
3. **Player displacement conflict**: occupant is bumped to Idle silently.
4. **Court overflow**: courts grid becomes vertically scrollable when courts exceed visible height.
5. **Singles/Doubles mismatch**: a queue card with 4 slots dragged to a 2-slot singles court fills the first 2 slots, ignoring the rest.

## Acceptance Criteria
1. Default state loads with 3 doubles courts and no players on a fresh session.
2. Dragging a player to a slot sets their status to `playing` immediately.
3. Finishing a match with "Team A wins" gives each Team A player `+1 win` and each Team B player `+1 loss`, then returns all to Idle.
4. "Clear Session" resets to the 3-court default after 2-click confirmation.
5. Removing a court returns its players to Idle and renumbers the display.
6. Level chips remain legible (opaque, correct contrast) on the green court surface.

## Constraints
1. A match cannot be "Finished" unless there is at least 1 player on each side.
2. A player cannot occupy two courts or a court and queue simultaneously — enforced by the store's `releaseFromCourts` / `releaseFromQueue` helpers.
3. Layout: courts grid is horizontally contained in `minmax(0,1fr)`; scrollable vertically.
4. Finished match stats cannot be undone without manually editing player profiles via the Edit dialog.
5. Court numbers are auto-assigned as `max(existing numbers) + 1` and do not re-sequence on deletion.
